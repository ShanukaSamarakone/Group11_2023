Mathematics

Addition

Addition is combining two or more numbers to find their total.

Example: If you have 3 apples and you get 2 more apples, how many apples do you have in total?

Explanation:
- You start with 3 apples.
- Then you get 2 more apples.
- Now count all the apples: 1, 2, 3, 4, 5.
- So, 3 apples + 2 apples = 5 apples.
- Therefore, you have 5 apples in total.

Addition

Subtraction

Subtraction is taking away one number from another to find the difference.

Example: If you have 5 apples and you give away 2 apples, how many apples do you have in left?

Explanation:
- You start with 5 apples.
- Then you give away 2 apples.
- Now count how many apples are left: 1, 2, 3.
- So, 5 apples - 2 apples = 3 apples.
- Therefore, you have 3 apples left.

Subtraction

Multiplication

Multiplication is adding a number to itself a certain number of times.

Example: If you have 3 groups of 4 apples, how many apples do you have in total?

Explanation:
- You have 3 groups.
- Each group has 4 apples.
- Multiply 3 (groups) by 4 (apples in each group) to find the total.
- So, 3 groups of 4 apples is 3 x 4 = 12
- Therefore, you have 12 apples in total.

Multiplication

Division

Division is splitting a number into equal parts or groups.

Example: If you have 10 apples and you want to divide them equally among 2 friends, how many apples does each friend get?

Explanation:
- You start with 10 apples.
- You want to divide these apples equally among 2 friends.
- To find out how many apples each friend gets, you divide 10 apples by 2.
- So, 10 apples รท 2 friends = 5 apples per friend.
- Therefore, each friend gets 5 apples.

Fraction

A fraction represents a part of a whole or, more generally, any number of equal parts.

Example: Imagine you have a pizza that is cut into 4 equal slices. If you eat 1 slice, how much of the pizza have you eaten?

Explanation:
- The whole pizza is divided into 4 equal parts (slices).
- You eat one of these parts.
- This part you ate is represented as 1/4 of the pizza.
- So, you have eaten 1 out of the 4 slices.
- Therefore, you have eaten a fraction, 1/4, of the whole pizza.

Fraction

Percentages

A percentage is a way of expressing a number as a fraction of 100. It is denoted using the symbol "%".

Example: If you have a bag of 100 marbles, and 25 of those marbles are red, what percentage of the marbles are red?

Explanation:
- You have a total of 100 marbles.
- Out of these, 25 marbles are red.
- To find the percentage of red marbles, you divide the number of red marbles (25) by the total number of marbles (100) and then multiply by 100.
- So, (25 รท 100) x 100 = 25%
- Therefore, 25% of the marbles in the bag are red.

English

Full Stop

A full stop ( . ) is used to indicate the end of a declarative sentence or statement.

Example: I have a cat.

Explanation:
- The full stop at the end of the sentence signals that the statement is complete.

- It tells the reader to pause before moving on to the next sentence.

Full Stop

Comma

A comma ( , ) indicates a short pause in a sentence. It is used to separate items in a list, clauses, and adjectives, and to set off introductory elements.

Example: I bought apples, oranges, and bananas.

Explanation:
- The commas separate the items in the list, making the sentence easier to read and understand.

Comma

Question Mark

A question mark ( ? ) is used at the end of a sentence to indicate a direct question.

Example: Are you going to the party tonight?.

Explanation:
- The question mark replaces the period at the end of a sentence when the sentence is a question.

- It signals to the reader that an answer or response is expected.

Question Mark

Exclamation Mark

An exclamation mark ( ! ) is used at the end of a sentence to express strong feelings or a high volume (shouting) or to emphasize a statement.

Example: Watch out!

Explanation:
- The exclamation mark indicates that the sentence is an urgent warning or command.

- It conveys a sense of alarm or urgency that a period or question mark cannot.

- This punctuation mark tells the reader that the speaker is either shouting or speaking very forcefully.

Exclamation Mark

English Continued

Quotation Marks

Quotation marks (" ") are used to enclose direct speech, quotations, or a phrase to indicate that the words are used in a special or ironic sense.

Example: She said, "Meet me at noon."

Explanation:
- The quotation marks enclose the words directly spoken by the person.

- They signal to the reader that these are the exact words the person used.

Quotation Marks

Parentheses

Parentheses ( ) are used to include additional information or clarification that is not essential to the main point of the sentence.

Example: He finally answered (after taking five minutes to think) that he did not understand the question.

Explanation:
- This use of parentheses shows how they can add non-essential, yet interesting or important details without interrupting the flow of the main sentence.

Parentheses

Semicolon

A semicolon ( ; ) is used to link two independent clauses that are closely related in thought but could stand as separate sentences.

Example: I have a big test tomorrow; I can't go out tonight.

Explanation:
- Both parts of the sentence could be separate sentences, but the semicolon shows they are closely connected.

- This use of a semicolon helps the reader see the relationship between the two clauses without starting a new sentence.

Semicolon

Colon

A colon ( : ) is used to introduce a list, a quotation or an explanation.

Example: There are three primary colors: red, yellow, and blue.

Explanation:
- The colon introduces a list of the three primary colors.

- It signals to the reader that what follows the colon is directly related to what comes before it.
